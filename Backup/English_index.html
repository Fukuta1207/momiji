PROFILE

Brief Bio

I am a graduate student in theoretical particle physics, working mainly on gauge theories, spontaneous symmetry breaking, and topological defects such as magnetic monopoles. I am particularly interested in the structure and stability of monopole configurations in multi-stage symmetry breaking scenarios and their relation to charge quantisation.

This website collects a short description of my research topics, preprints and publications, and records of talks and seminars. In the future, I plan to upload slides, lecture notes, and some technical memos.

RESEARCH

Research Topics

A brief overview of my current research projects. This section will be updated as the work progresses.

01 / MONOPOLES

Monopoles in multi-stage symmetry breaking

I study monopole solutions arising in multi-stage gauge symmetry breaking \( G \rightarrow H \rightarrow \cdots \), focusing on the structure of the magnetic charge lattice and its relation to low-energy effective descriptions.

02 / GAUGE THEORY

Gauge groups, homotopy, and 1-form symmetries

I am interested in how homotopy groups and generalized global symmetries (in particular 1-form symmetries) encode charge quantisation and the classification of topological charges in concrete gauge-theory models.

03 / COSMOLOGY

Electroweak-scale cosmology and defects

I explore possible roles of topological defects in early-Universe cosmology, including inflation and electroweak baryogenesis, using effective potentials and field-theoretic dynamics.
